After 23 years of buying and architecting enterprise software, I've started building it instead.

As Group Chief Architect at Richemont, and in enterprise-architecture leadership roles at Landmark Group, Alshaya Group and Microsoft, I spent over two decades on the buy side. I watched the same two things happen over and over: transformation plans that never became real, and software we rented at enormous cost but never truly owned.

Capable AI has changed what's possible. So I'm doing the thing I used to advise on — building AI-native enterprise software, owned outright, in the open.

I recently became a Claude Certified Architect and trained my team to the same bar, because I don't want to write about this shift from the sidelines. I want to write about it from inside the build.

So I've started a newsletter — From Buyer to Builder — for CIOs, CTOs, CPOs and IT leaders navigating the same shift. No hype, no vendor gloss. Just what I'm learning as I build.
